 music
+ the hyperreal music archives is one of the net's best sites for finding out about electronic, experimental, and ambient music.

+ the ultimate band list is pretty slim on techno, but great for most everything else.


jungle/breakbeat
+ by far the best and most comprehensive breaks-related site is breaks from england. also, for those interested in a serious analysis of the cultural/historical origins of jungle, the breaks site features an essential (if perpetually unfinished) history entitled journey into da jungle, compiled by our friend harvey tyler.

+ a far less-comprehensive jungle site is steve shapiro's the jungle pages. its information has not been updated much but the site provides some great commentary and analysis (both from steve and from the artists themselves).

+ junglized

+ mission global junglism is a site from brooklyn ny that has recently expanded quite a bit, including jungle event listings, interviews with djs and musicians, record reviews, and more.

+ andrew gianni's breaks...the technical side offers a concise "manual" of jungle composition.

+ suburban base now have their own site as well.

+ the unofficial home of reinforced.


detroit techno
+ the 313 list and website on hyperreal features discussion about detroit and detroit-related techno music...a must for the hardcore fan.

+ submerge is underground resistance's distribution company and umbrella concept, featuring the store somewhere in detroit, which offers all submerge & ur releases as well as those for red planet, metroplex, generator, direct beat, 430 west, and several other labels. the building in which submerge was housed is also part of the city's proposed area 1, which is due to eventually be demolished to make way for a baseball stadium.

+ 7th city is dan bell's distribution company, now housed off of hyperreal. 7th city exclusively distributes many of detroit's up-and-coming labels, as well as some well-established ones and a few from beyond the detroit area. + planet e communications is carl craig's brainchild, bringing you quality releases from detroit and beyond.

+ nude is dale lawrence's online mag from detroit featuring articles on detroit techno and related subjects.

+ transmat, derrick may's pioneering techno label


techno
+ r & s records' website is very beautiful and contains some great information on one of the world's most influential techno/trance labels, whose musical tastes are now expanding into other areas.

+ swim~ records, begun by the band wire's own colin newman along with his wife malka spigel of minimal compact, has released some of the most interesting music of the last year or so, and their site is well worth exploring.

+ mixmaster morris oversees a site for sore ears, focusing on ambient music.

+ the plus 8 site: an exercise in minimalism...


other interesting music sites worth exploring
+ negativland offers some tasty morsels--pastries, desserts, and a healthy dose of copyright information and other media manipulation. you're just not on the web unless you get a taste of negativeland

+ in the same spirit, our friends over at the evolution control committee offer multi-media fun for the whole family!

+ get out your hankies for this one: the capt. james t. kirk sing-a-long page, now housed on a new site.

+ crash worship: if you haven't seen them or heard them, look here to understand (under re-construction).

girly stuff
feminism on the web
+ guerrila girls is a site with attitude. if you like to see female empowerment at work then check this out. women lashing back at the system, through public art, and with a sense of humor.

+ for a totally different look at many popular/current films, look at the feminist film reviews site. a good step in responding to the male-dominated hollywood paradigm.


girly sites with a mission

+ anonymous is the online version of everybody's favorite rave 'zine!

+ the lost issue of sassy: sissy


literary/counterculture (dont yawn, it's cool stuff!)
+ literary kicks offers the most complete information on the beat generation, including biographies, literary criticism, and other information about this groundbreaking group of authors, poets, and artists. it goes far beyond black berets, cafes, and sunglasses.

+ the wild bohemian home page is about exactly what you think it is. a friendly place for kindred spirits.

+ william s. burroughs is among the most important authors of our time, whose paranoid, techno-horror-druggged-apocalyptic-nightmare visions have influenced several generations of writers and musicians (from bowie in the 70s to sonic youth and many electronic musicians of today). the unofficial william s. burroughs homepage and the william s. burroughs internet webzone (housed on hyperreal) are both good introductions towards an understanding of his literary and artistic contributions.

+ seeker1's cyberanthropology page (currently unavailable) is a great page if you are interested in exploring the wider connections between culture, technology, and the implications this all has on us now and in the future. this is a place to spend some time reading...
