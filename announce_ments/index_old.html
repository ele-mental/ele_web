[30 november 01]

DARKSIDE 9: THE 9TH CIRCLE SELF-REVIEW
after a rather low-key 8th event in november 2000 featuring KID 606 and other TIGERBEAT 6 artists, the DARKSIDE series returns to form with a 9th installment, entitled THE 9TH CIRCLE, coordinated by DYSKINESIA and ELE_MENTAL's edward luna.

housed at BLD studio, a former lumber mill turned low-tech studio space, DARKSIDE begins with a capable set of dark ambience by SEVEN e-zine's impresario, ANTON. smoothly mixing a CD-only set that ranges from abused atmosphere to broken beats, anton sets the stage nicely for the rest of the event. GREG TRASH (aka AMERICAN TRASH from akron) follows with a short set of live breakbeat noise on two computers.

ELUNA continues with a short DJ set of noisy electro before being joined by MARCUS MATTHEWS (formerly of the band MAKESHIFT CONQUEST) on guitar, and TIM RAMAGE (of THE LACK and BLOW UP) on guitar and keyboard, creating an onslaught of sound that blurs the distinctions between techno music and live instrumentation.

the real surprise of the night is pittsburgh's XANOPTICON, who proceeds to tear up the joint with his live, unhinged breakcore set. his performance adds a well needed element of presence to the computer music scene, as he frantically engages with his equipment.

world-renowned break-core troublemaker VENETIAN SNARES (aka aaron funk from winnipeg, canada) follows suit with a set of his own latest CDR creations. snares had been scheduled to appear with warp/rephlex artist MU-ZIQ at the wexner center for the arts until that tour was cancelled, so naturally we were happy to have the creator of such fine works as "doll doll doll" in our midst. and he did not disappoint.

the event ends with a tag-team set of strangeness from DJs APOSTOLIC and YUUKIMAIKO, representing local noisecore crew DYSKINESIA.

[17 november 01]
MALFUNCTION RAVE EVENT BUSTED;
COLUMBUS SCENE UNDER THREAT
in what amounts to a swan song for columbus' long-fledgling underground rave scene, an event by columbus stalwarts MALFUNCTION is shut down at 1:30 AM on the evening of the 17th. using three full floors of the same warehouse that was used for a smaller, one room event in july (TRANSIT), the ravers and the music attract too much attention from the authorities this time. after arriving at the space at about 1 AM, the police are quick to threaten the promoters with various citations if they do not shut down immediately. luckily, FABRICE LIG, coming all the way from belgium, still manages to perform his rare live set to a group of eager listeners.

even so, one can't help wonder what this means for the future of true undeground electronic music events in columbus. ele_mental's own solution (for the time being) has been to use a stable space like the dragonfy annex for recurring events, but other alternatives should exist in order to ensure a healthy scene.

i myself find the columbus police department's policies inconsistent and disturbing. on the one hand, they have frequently harrassed (or left unprotected) alternative spaces for creative expression, such as FIREXIT, ADDITION 13, LEGION OF DOOM; but at the same time they are slow to respond to much more serious recreational drug and alcohol abuse found in mainstream clubs and campus keg parties.

a more rational alterntative (and one used by european and canadian law enforcement agencies until recently) is a policy of engagement and selective enforcement.

instead of immediately shutting the party down, sending dozens or hundreds of kids onto streets and highways in the middle of the night (often on drugs), the CPD should find ways of negotiating with the promoters on the spot, to reasonably ensure everyone's safety while making an honest assessment of the situation at hand. if a clear threat to public safety exists (such as an obvious fire hazard), then the authorities should have the right to enforce the law; if it is merely a few dozen kids dancing in a warehouse all night, and no threat exists, let the party continue!

if anyone cares to make their own views on this matter public, please call the CPD internal affairs bureau at 645-4880, or write to columbus mayor michal coleman's action center.
--edward luna

COMPOUND 003 BRINGS SAFETY SCISSORS
SAFETY SCISSORS, aka mattew curry, returns to columbus fresh from his recent move to berlin, germany. his set is a pleasant mix of experimental glitch techno with some live vocals and rock'n'roll posturing, including a brilliantly stripped-down cover of NEW ORDER's "age of consent" (!). he ends the evening with a short DJ set featuring some favorites from his back catalogue of fellow glitchy techno producers.

before SAFETY SCISSORS, charles noel (aka ARCHETYPE) lays down a set of live, shuffly, tribal techno that has become his trademark. as if it needs to be repeated, charles' releases for 21-22 CORPORATION, GLYPH, BLACK NATION, and ONGAKU have slowly transformed techno (and even house) music. charles is preceded on the decks by our longtime collaborator THOMAS JAMES (of RESIDUAL RECORDINGS), with a set of low-key tech-house; and the event kicks off with an energetic set of ambient/electro/noise by LUNAR 1970 (aka ed luna).

cleveland's PHASE 4 and columbus' DAVID TINAPPLE again provide the brilliant treated visuals that always provide the COMPOUND events with their unique visual flavor. the event is again hosted by ele_mental @ the DRAGONFLY ANNEX space, with AARON ALVIN SCHROEDER'S interactive mechanical sculptures and space-age dome still in place. thanks to todd sines for once again pulling together an excellent event, reeling in about 100 people to support the cause of good music and art here in columbus.
--ele_mental
