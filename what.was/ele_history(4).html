ele_history (4). . .



all of this culminated in one of our most successful events, called snow*fall on 2 feb 96, a "sequel" of sorts to snow*ball from over 2 years earlier. great moments included another scorching live music set by todd and one by stephen, mc'd by detroiters brian gillespie and paris mack. paris, along with fellow detroiter jon williams (billeebob), also made his triumphant return to columbus, marking a milestone in the columbus-detroit collaboration. anthony shakir joined us as well, showing off his skills in an explosive dj set. other highlights: placebo and doughboy's well-recieved trax tag team, gaga's exploratory ambient/industrial set in the 2nd room, brian's hip-hop/lounge set (also in the 2nd room), and much more. especially satisfying (for me) was the high attendance (over 550!!) who came out in 5-degree weather to support us. perhaps it was only a question of timing, but i felt as though this event proved that our message was finally beginning to be understood.

although the smaller event like snow*fall seem to be the best way to send the message, several bigger events have already happened (including doughboy's massive generator event with the UK's dave clarke, in april 96, not to mention the latest gargantuan event called METAMORPHOSIS along with collective intelligence, which attracted upwards of 3500 people in sept 97). you can bet, however, that even our bigger events will continue to poke and pry at what the definition of a "rave" is. to me, it's a place where one has the mental space to lose yourself, while at the same time feeling safe enough to ask the deeper questions about yourself and the world around you. it's about combining mental aspects of traditional artistic experiences with the bodily aspects of the party experience.

look under what will be to find out what we have planned next.

charles continues to work on his own music and on the next releases for the 21/22 corp. todd has periodically worked with charles on a.r.s. and has released several records on peacefrog, one on dan bell's seventh city label, and even one on hyperactive's contact label outta chicago (released under the name trait), and in 1997 he has debuted his own company called core. titonton released an ep on metamorphic in late 1995, and has recently collaborated with morgan geist for a release on the phono label from the UK, and has many other projects in the works. stephen did music for the jungle sky label, and worked with r notorious j in new york for the rawkus label. other ele_mentals have had wide releases and continue to work on new material...(including kevin kennedy/powerhouse,kevin krahel/placebo, kevin ginger, and more...)

so finally, ele_mental has begun to truly grow into what its original vision was: a loose affiliation of people who see art/techno/life as part of the same thing.

when time allows i will update this history further, in even more detail. i have written it for myself and for the cru, but hopefully some of you that have read this far will contact us if you are down...there's always room for more people and more ideas ...
